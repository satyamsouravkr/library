Library Rules

  • Late submission of books will result in a fine of ₹50 per day.

    Late submission of library books will attract a fine of ₹50 per day, calculated from the due date until the book is returned. To maintain fairness and availability of resources for all users, we kindly request that books be returned on or before the due date. Repeated delays may lead to additional disciplinary actions.

  • If a book is damaged, you must pay the full cost.

    If a book is returned in a damaged condition, the borrower will be held responsible for the damage and must pay the full replacement cost of the book. This policy ensures that library materials remain in good condition for all users and helps maintain the overall quality of the library’s collection.

  • Books can be issued for up to 14 days, with one renewal allowed.

    Books can be issued to users for a maximum period of 14 days. If needed, a one-time renewal is permitted, allowing the user to extend the borrowing period. However, this renewal is subject to the book not being reserved by another user. Timely return after renewal is also strictly expected.

  • Library cards are non-transferable.

    Library cards are issued to individual users and are strictly non-transferable. Each card is intended for personal use only and must not be shared, lent, or used by others. Misuse of a library card, including allowing someone else to use it, may result in suspension of library privileges or other penalties.
